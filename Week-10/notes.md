# Week 10: The Defender

## Overview

Week 10 focused on Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR), shifting from offensive security operations to investigative and defensive practices. Through hands-on forensic investigations, I learned how to preserve evidence, conduct live system triage, analyze memory and disk artifacts, correlate security events, and reconstruct attack timelines. The week emphasized evidence-based analysis, chain of custody procedures, and incident response methodologies used by professional forensic investigators.

## Skills Developed

* Incident Response (PICERL) methodology
* Live system triage
* Evidence collection and preservation
* Chain of custody procedures
* MD5 and SHA256 hashing
* Memory forensics
* Disk forensics and file recovery
* The Sleuth Kit (TSK)
* Log analysis and correlation
* SIEM investigation techniques
* Attack timeline reconstruction
* Forensic reporting and documentation

## Labs Completed

### Day 1: The Crime Scene

Performed live incident response on a potentially compromised system. Collected volatile system information, identified active threats, and generated cryptographic hashes to preserve forensic evidence and maintain chain of custody.

**Artifact:** `collection_log.txt`

### Day 2: The Digital Autopsy

Conducted memory and disk forensic analysis to investigate a suspected malware infection. Recovered forensic artifacts from memory, analyzed disk evidence, and documented findings related to the compromise.

**Artifact:** `forensic_findings.md`

### Day 3: The Central Nervous System

Used a SIEM platform to investigate a simulated enterprise compromise. Correlated logs across multiple systems, identified indicators of compromise, and reconstructed an attack timeline using event data.

**Artifact:** `attack_timeline.csv`

### Day 4: Operation Phantom Pursuit

Completed a full incident response investigation by combining SIEM analysis, live system triage, evidence preservation, and forensic recovery techniques. Documented the attack lifecycle, identified the source of compromise, secured evidence, and recovered deleted malicious artifacts.

**Artifact:** `Incident_Response_Report.md`

## Key Takeaways

This week provided practical experience in digital forensics and incident response by focusing on evidence collection, forensic analysis, and attack reconstruction. Through memory analysis, disk investigations, SIEM correlation, and chain of custody procedures, I developed the skills needed to investigate security incidents, preserve evidence integrity, and produce professional forensic reports that support incident response and remediation efforts.
