# Week 8: The Breach

## Overview

Week 8 focused on the exploitation and post-exploitation phases of the cyber attack lifecycle. Building on reconnaissance and vulnerability assessment skills from the previous week, I explored how security professionals validate vulnerabilities, assess privilege escalation risks, evaluate persistence mechanisms, and understand lateral movement within enterprise environments. The week also reinforced the importance of defensive detection through security automation and forensic analysis.

## Skills Developed

* Vulnerability validation and verification
* Metasploit Framework fundamentals
* Reverse shell concepts
* Linux privilege escalation analysis
* Windows privilege escalation assessment
* Persistence mechanisms
* Network pivoting concepts
* Post-exploitation methodology
* Authentication log analysis
* Python-based security automation
* Threat detection and forensic reporting

Validated discovered vulnerabilities and demonstrated exploitation within a controlled lab environment. Used industry-standard security tools to confirm security weaknesses and document successful access.

## Key Takeaways

This week provided insight into how attackers move from initial access to deeper system compromise and how defenders can detect those activities. By studying exploitation, privilege escalation, persistence, and post-exploitation techniques in controlled environments, I gained a stronger understanding of attack paths and the defensive controls needed to detect and mitigate them. The week concluded with the development of a Python-based detection tool, reinforcing the importance of combining offensive knowledge with defensive capabilities.
